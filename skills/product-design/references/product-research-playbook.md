# Product Research Playbook

Use this reference when product advice needs external examples. Keep the work
proportional to the decision.

## Pick References

Choose 3-6 references:

- 2-3 direct alternatives or competitors
- 1-2 adjacent products with a similar workflow
- 1 indie or smaller-scope product when available

Prefer sources that show actual product behavior:

- official product pages
- public demos
- docs and help articles
- screenshots or app store listings
- pricing pages
- changelogs and release notes
- credible user reviews

Avoid relying only on listicles, SEO comparison pages, or vague commentary.

## Observe

For each reference, capture only what matters to the question:

- target user
- product promise
- entry point or onboarding
- core workflow
- feature shape
- pricing or packaging if relevant
- friction, complexity, or missing states
- any pattern that seems intentionally constrained or focused

## Synthesize

After inspecting references, summarize:

- common pattern: what most products do
- strong exception: one product that does something differently
- hidden cost: complexity the user may not want
- personal-product fit: what the user can borrow without becoming too heavy

## Recommend

End with a right-sized recommendation:

- what to build, test, decide, or research next
- one thing to avoid for now
- what evidence would change the recommendation

Do not present competitor behavior as proof. Treat it as evidence that helps
form a hypothesis.
